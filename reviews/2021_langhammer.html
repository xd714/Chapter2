Detailed Experimental Design Flow
1
Historical Foundation and Line Development
Long-term selection experiment spanning multiple decades
Timeline: Started 1970s, >45 years of selection
Founder population: FZTDU (4 inbred + 4 outbred lines)
Selection criterion: 1.6 × litter size + total birth weight (g)
Generation interval: Short (single litter per dam)
2
Lifetime Fecundity Study Design
Comprehensive lifetime reproductive assessment protocol
Sample sizes: FL1 (50), FL2 (60), CTRL (50), DU6 (30), DU6P (30)
Mating protocol: 1:1 ratio, 2-week mating + 3-week observation
Standardization: Litters adjusted to 10 pups post-birth
Weaning: 21 days, then immediate re-mating
3
Breeding Protocol and Buck Rotation
Systematic approach to minimize male effects
Buck selection: Same line as female
Rotation system: 3-4 rounds per buck maximum
Failed conception: Second mating attempt with different buck
Removal criteria: No offspring after 2 mating rounds
4
Health Monitoring and Record Keeping
Comprehensive health and performance tracking
Daily observation: Health status assessment
Weight recording: Female weight at each mating round
Gestation tracking: Length recorded for each litter
Removal reasons: Fertility failure vs health problems documented
5
Data Collection and Measurements
Systematic measurement protocol throughout lifetime
Litter traits: Size, birth weight, standardization records
Temporal data: Mating to birth intervals, gestation length
Survival data: Age at removal, reason for termination
Reproductive success: Number of successful matings per female
6
Statistical Analysis Framework
Advanced survival and mixed model analysis
Software: R 3.5.2 and SAS 9.4
Models: GLIMMIX (Poisson), LIFETEST (Kaplan-Meier)
Comparisons: Tukey-Kramer multiple comparisons
Fixed effects: Line, litter number, group effects

Why, How, What Analysis
WHY - Research Motivation
Economic Importance: Lifetime fecundity more relevant than single-litter performance for agricultural applications.
  • Agricultural relevance: Farm animals valued for lifetime productivity
  • Sustainability focus: Long-term reproductive performance vs short-term gains
  • Model validation: Test whether first-litter selection improves lifetime outcomes
  • Strategy comparison: Different genetic approaches to high fertility
Knowledge Gap: Limited understanding of long-term consequences of fertility selection in mammals.
HOW - Methodology Innovation
Comprehensive Approach: First systematic lifetime fecundity comparison of long-term selected mouse lines.
  • Long-term selection: >181 generations of documented selection
  • Multiple strategies: Compare FL1 vs FL2 approaches
  • Control comparisons: Include unselected and negative controls
  • Health monitoring: Distinguish fertility vs health-related removal
Validation Protocol: Standardized breeding protocol eliminates environmental confounding factors.
WHAT - Key Discoveries
Major Findings: Two distinct strategies for achieving high fertility with different lifetime outcomes.
  • FL1 Strategy: Balanced - highest lifetime productivity (101.2 Silver index)
  • FL2 Strategy: Intensive - highest single litters but reduced lifetime
  • Health correlation: High fertility associated with better general health
  • Trade-offs revealed: Maximum vs sustainable fertility strategies
Practical Impact: Provides framework for evaluating breeding strategies in agricultural species.

Agricultural Applications
Breeding Program Guidelines

For Livestock Breeding:

  • Selection strategy: Balance single-litter performance with lifetime productivity
  • Health monitoring: Track reproductive longevity alongside fertility
  • Economic evaluation: Consider total lifetime value, not just peak performance
  • Sustainability focus: Avoid excessive selection pressure that reduces lifespan

Model Applications:

  • Pig breeding: FL1 strategy translates to sustainable sow productivity
  • Dairy cattle: Lifetime milk yield optimization principles
  • Poultry: Egg production longevity vs peak laying rates
  • Sheep/Goats: Reproductive lifespan in extensive systems
Selection Trade-offs Revealed
Critical Finding: Intensity vs Sustainability
The study reveals a fundamental trade-off in reproductive selection: FL2's intensive selection for maximum litter size resulted in reduced reproductive lifespan and ultimately lower lifetime productivity compared to FL1's balanced approach. This has major implications for commercial breeding programs where lifetime economic value is more important than peak performance.
