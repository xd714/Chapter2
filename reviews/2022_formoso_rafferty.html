Detailed Experimental Design Flow
1
Historical Selection Background
Long-term divergent selection experiment for birth weight variability
Selection duration: 23 generations of divergent selection
Starting generation: Generation 20 and 21 used for longevity study
Selection criterion: Environmental variability (residual variance)
Mating design: Weighted selection determined by simulated annealing
2
Reproductive Longevity Study Design
Comprehensive lifetime reproductive performance assessment
Sample size: 172 females total (86 from each line)
Generations tested: 20th and 21st generation
Mating protocol: One male to one female, stayed together permanently
Termination criteria: 63 days elapsed from last parturition
3
Data Collection Protocol
Systematic recording of reproductive events and performance
Parturition monitoring: Daily checks during parturition period
Litter traits: Litter size at birth (LSB) and weaning (LSW)
Timing data: Interval between parturitions (IBP)
Longevity measure: Length of reproductive life (LRL)
4
Statistical Analysis Framework
Advanced survival analysis and mixed model approaches
Linear mixed models: LSB, LSW, IBP comparisons
Cox proportional hazard: Length of reproductive life analysis
Prentice-Williams-Peterson: Rate of parturitions model
Software: SAS for mixed models, R survival package

Why, How, What Analysis
WHY - Research Motivation
Critical Knowledge Gap: Understanding the relationship between uniformity and robustness in reproductive performance.
  • Uniformity benefits: Previously shown for litter size, survival, feed efficiency
  • Robustness indicators: Need to assess long-term reproductive fitness
  • Agricultural relevance: Longevity economically important in livestock production
  • Model validation: Test whether birth weight uniformity affects lifetime performance
Hypothesis: Selection for birth weight uniformity (low variability) will improve reproductive longevity and robustness.
HOW - Methodology Innovation
Comprehensive Survival Analysis: First systematic study of reproductive longevity in birth weight variability selected lines.
  • Divergent selection: 23 generations of selection for opposite traits
  • Lifetime assessment: Continuous mating until reproductive failure
  • Advanced statistics: Cox models + Prentice-Williams-Peterson models
  • Multiple measures: Time to reproductive end AND rate of new parturitions
Validation: Robust experimental design with appropriate controls and statistical power.
WHAT - Key Discoveries
Dramatic Reproductive Advantages: Low variability line shows superior lifetime performance across all measures.
  • 4.5√ó longer reproductive life: 252.5 vs 55.5 days median
  • 3√ó higher parturition rate: HR = 2.93 for new parturitions
  • Larger, more frequent litters: +2.75 pups, shorter intervals
  • Robustness confirmed: Uniformity strongly linked to reproductive fitness
Practical Impact: Demonstrates uniformity as a valuable selection criterion for improving both productivity and sustainability.

Research Implications and Applications
Agricultural Applications

Livestock Breeding Strategy:

  • Selection criteria: Include within-litter uniformity as breeding objective
  • Sustainability focus: Prioritize longevity alongside productivity
  • Risk reduction: Uniform offspring reduce mortality and management costs
  • Economic benefits: Longer productive life = reduced replacement costs

Model Species Applications:

  • Pig breeding: Address increased birth weight variability from litter size selection
  • Rabbit production: Apply uniformity principles to improve doe longevity
  • Poultry breeding: Consider uniformity in hatching weight optimization
  • Dairy cattle: Evaluate uniformity effects on productive lifespan
